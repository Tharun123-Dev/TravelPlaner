10 of the best road trips to take in the USA

Wide-open landscapes, coastal drives and dramatic canyons — pick a route and start planning.

1. PACIFIC COAST HIGHWAY

Best road trip for Pacific views — Start/finish: San Diego – Seattle · Approx. distance: 1600 miles · Time: 8–12 days

The Pacific Coast Highway stitches together the West Coast's most notable cities and coastal scenery. Expect cliff-top views, sea-spray air and charming seaside towns. This is a route for taking your time and stopping for viewpoints along the way.

2. BLUE RIDGE PARKWAY

Best Appalachian road trip — Start/finish: Cherokee, NC – Waynesboro, VA · Approx. distance: 469 miles · Time: 2–5 days

The Blue Ridge Parkway delivers rolling green mountains, forested overlooks and many hiking opportunities. In autumn the route is awash with colour — ideal for scenic drives, short walks and viewpoint stops.

3. ROUTE 66

Best road trip for American kitsch — Start/finish: Chicago – Los Angeles · Approx. distance: 2250 miles · Time: 1–2 weeks

Nicknamed the Mother Road, Route 66 crosses diverse landscapes, taking you through small towns, neon signs and wacky roadside attractions. Drive it in sections or all at once — there are plenty of retro diners and oddities to discover.

4. NATCHETZ TRACE PARKWAY

Best road trip for Southern history — Start/finish: Pasquo, TN – Natchez, MS · Approx. distance: 444 miles · Time: 2–3 days

The Natchez Trace Parkway follows an ancient trail linking historical towns, grand plantations and tranquil woodlands. It’s a calm, scenic drive with cultural stops and gentle pace — perfect for combining small-town visits and scenic detours.

5. BADLANDS–BLACK HILLS LOOP

Best road trip in the Great Plains — Start/finish: Badlands National Park · Approx. distance: 330 miles · Time: 2 days

From stark canyons to rolling grassland, this loop showcases geological oddities and cultural landmarks like Mount Rushmore. The region’s dramatic vistas reward the driver who wants wide-open spaces and memorable sunset views.

6. FLORIDA HIGHWAY 1

Best tropical road trip — Key West & the Florida Keys · Approx. distance: varied · Time: a few days to a week

Highway 1 threads across islands and bridges with ocean on both sides. Expect warm water, marine life and casual roadside stops. It’s a short drive that feels like an escape to the tropics.

7. GRAND CIRCLE

Best for red-rock canyons & national parks — Utah & northern Arizona loop

This loop gives access to some of the Southwest’s most famous parks and scenic drives: think red cliffs, expansive canyons and extraordinary desert scenery. Plan for hikes and viewpoints to make the most of the trip.

8. THE GREAT RIVER ROAD

Best river scenery — Along the Mississippi River

Follow the Mississippi through river towns, levees and cultural stops. The Great River Road reveals the heartland’s history, music traditions and riverside vistas — a mellow route with lots of local character.

9. THE ALASKA HIGHWAY

Best remote road trip — Vast wilderness and wildlife

Long distances and remote services make this a trip for prepared travelers. Expect incredible wildlife sightings, alpine views and true wilderness roads. Allow extra time for weather and road conditions.

10. CABOT TRAIL

Best coastal cliffs — Cape Breton scenic drive (example)

Winding coastal roads, dramatic headlands and picnic spots — this drive is especially beautiful in autumn when the colours change. Stop for short hikes and lookouts for sweeping sea views.

11. PARKWAY & MOUNTAIN DRIVE

Great scenic detours

A collection of scenic drives through mountains, with winding roads and lookout points — great for shorter day trips and photo stops.

12. COASTAL & BEACH ROUTES

Great for seaside towns

A selection of coastal drives that highlight beaches, lighthouses and relaxed towns. These are perfect if you prefer short stops and seafood restaurants along the way.

13. DESERT EXPRESS

Best for desert light and stars

Desert drives let you experience sky, silence and wide-open horizons. Travel at dawn or dusk for dramatic shading and great photography.

14. LAKESIDE ROUTES

Quiet drives by water

Lake loops and riverside drives are ideal for calm getaways — bring boots for short walks and a picnic for the shore.

15. NATIONAL PARK CLUSTER

Hop between parks

Plan a route that links several parks for an intense nature trip — best when you allow time for hikes and exploration.

16. URBAN HOPPER

City-to-city route

If you love museums, food and architecture, take a city-hopping trip between major urban centers, with short countryside stretches between them.

17. SUNSET & VIEWPOINT ROUTE

Best for evening drives

Curate a short route with several viewpoints for sunset or sunrise photography — short, memorable and easy to plan.
